# Triage and urgency reference (for RAG retrieval)

## General principles
- Prioritize patient safety. When in doubt, recommend higher acuity or urgent evaluation.
- Consider patient history: recent surgery, chronic conditions, and medications can change urgency.
- Chest pain, difficulty breathing, and severe abdominal pain warrant urgent or emergency evaluation.

## Symptom–urgency cues
- Chest pain: often requires emergency or cardiology evaluation; consider cardiac cause until ruled out.
- Rash / skin: typically lower urgency unless widespread, febrile, or rapidly spreading; suggest dermatology or primary care.
- Dental / tooth: urgent care or dentistry; escalate if swelling, fever, or difficulty swallowing.
- Abdominal pain: severity and location matter; severe or worsening pain suggests emergency evaluation.
- Shortness of breath: high priority; consider emergency or urgent care.
- Itching without systemic symptoms: usually primary care or dermatology; low urgency unless anaphylaxis suspected.

## Canadian Emergency Department Triage (CTAS-style)
- Level 1–2: Resuscitation / emergent – immediate care.
- Level 3: Urgent – care within 30 minutes.
- Level 4: Less urgent – care within 1 hour.
- Level 5: Non-urgent – care within 2 hours.

Use these as reference only; the triage engine applies rule-based logic with patient-specific context.
